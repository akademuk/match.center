Variety of Sports, Markets & Bets

Btting sites also get a rate for the markets they offer. There are 3 criteria that helps evaluate this:

  • Number of sports available. The more options a bookmaker has to offer, the easier it is for a customer to pick a necessary bet. There are around 20-25 types of sport offered on average. If there are obviously less sports than the number above a betting site loses points, otherwise it gets more. Best betting sites in the UK offer up to 40 sports to bet on.
  • Number of markets offered for top football events. This sport is very popular in the UK, so every bookmaker accepts football bets. Companies may show their advantages via a number and a variety of markets offered for this sport.
  • Margin. This is the thing that determines a bookmaker's guaranteed income. This is some kind of a customer’s fee. Margin was assessed based on two big markets - over/under and handicaps. The companies were further divided into three categories: low margin (up to 2.99%), medium margin (up to 4.99%), and high margin. The best rating goes to the companies with a low margin.

The next step after the margin evaluation is to assess the UK sports betting sites based on what odds they offer.

Odds

We take margin into account when we evaluate betting odds. The margin determines a bookmaker’s fee. If the margin is low the odds are more attractive for the customers.

Every betting site has markets with low, medium, or high odds. This highly depends on the level of a tournament, the popularity of a market, and the possibility to find enough information to make the right prediction. Events with high margins are not good for customers as such a bet is risky. However, the winnings will be more, too.

You need to choose what level of risk fits you more. High odds are not always a reason to place a bet. This is why the rating doesn’t contain a separate score for this parameter.

*Our table contains not only betting sites, but also websites of companies that operate as betting exchanges. Unlike sportsbooks, which set odds themselves, exchanges only coordinate transactions between customers. Therefore the range of betting markets on such websites is usually much shorter than in bookmaker companies, and contains only the most liquid markets. Betting exchanges have no margin and they earn in another way - by charging a commission on winnings. Please note that Betfair, a betting company, is also known as an exchange throughout the world.
